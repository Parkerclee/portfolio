When a [dashboard](/dashboard) flags who needs a nudge, the hard part isn't knowing, it's finding words that land without sounding like HR. Steal these.

**Someone stalled partway**
> Quick one, you're halfway through the data-handling module and it's due Friday. It's about six minutes. Knock it out when you get a sec and you're done: [link]

**Someone failed the check**
> Saw you finished the training, thanks. The PII section trips a lot of people up; want to grab 10 minutes this week so it's not just a checkbox? No big deal, I just want you covered.

**Someone flagged a risky habit in their answer**
> Thanks for being honest in that reflection. The thing you mentioned about exporting to a local file, let's find you a sanctioned way to do that so you're not exposed. Not a gotcha, promise.

**A great answer worth reinforcing**
> Your response on the breach question was exactly right. Mind walking through it in standup? Other folks would learn from how you thought about it.

**Re-engaging someone who's gone quiet**
> No agenda, I've just missed a bit of your spark lately and wanted to check in. Got 15 minutes this week?

**A digest for your team channel**
> Data Handling: we're 7 of 12 done with 3 days left. Two scores worth a quick chat (I'll DM you), four still to go. Thanks all, it's ~6 minutes if you haven't yet.

The whole rule: name the specific thing, offer help, drop the lecture.
