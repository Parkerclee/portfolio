## Overview

A seasonal lending product at a fintech company had training content that hadn't been updated in over a year. Agents were struggling — but leadership was attributing poor performance to "agent quality" rather than content gaps. There were no assessments, no data, and no way to prove training was the root cause. This project changed that.

---

## The Problem

Without evidence, the L&D team couldn't make the case for redesign. SME interviews alone wouldn't cut it — they'd produce subjective opinions, not proof. The existing training hadn't kept pace with operational reality, but no one could quantify exactly where or how badly it had drifted. Leadership's default: blame the agents.

---

## My Approach

I ran an **unconventional needs analysis**: instead of relying solely on SME interviews, I analyzed **400+ messages from agent communication channels** over a 10-month period, coding them into pain-point themes. This produced hard evidence of 6 dominant knowledge gaps — gaps the existing training either didn't cover or had covered with outdated information.

### From there:

**SOP coverage mapping** — Mapped 74 operational procedures against existing training content to quantify coverage gaps. The resulting matrix turned an argument from "I think the training is stale" into "here are the 23 procedures with zero training coverage."

**Context engine** — Built a structured reference database linking every procedure to its training module, assessment item, and knowledge base article, so gaps could be tracked systematically going forward. This became a maintenance tool the team continued using after the initial build.

**Blended curriculum design** — 5 eLearning modules, 2 instructor-led sessions, and 1 scenario-based debrief, organized around the agent's actual decision points — not the product's feature list.

**First-ever competency assessment** — Created with a full evaluation plan spanning reaction surveys through business impact metrics.

---

## Outcomes

- 🔄 Shifted the conversation from **"agents aren't good enough"** to **"the training hasn't kept up"** — backed by data leadership could act on
- 📚 Delivered a complete curriculum covering **~5 hours of blended instruction** and a 20-minute annual refresher
- 🔁 Established a **repeatable methodology**: communication analysis → SOP mapping → gap quantification → curriculum design
- 🛠️ The context engine became a maintenance tool the team continued using after the initial build

---

## What This Shows

Strategic needs analysis that goes beyond standard SME interviews. The ability to build a data-driven case for redesign without formal analytics tools or a learning data infrastructure. Assessment design from the ground up. Systems thinking: building tools that outlast the project.
