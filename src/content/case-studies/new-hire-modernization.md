## Overview

A credit decisioning team's new hire training was a 2-week lecture-heavy program where trainers read SOPs aloud. Assessments were closed-book knowledge checks that didn't reflect how the job actually worked. There were 100+ distinct job responsibilities across 116 procedures — and zero formal assessment gates before agents entered production. I redesigned it from the ground up.

---

## The Problem

The program taught *about* the job rather than training people *to do* the job. Learners sat through lectures on procedures they'd immediately forget, then passed a closed-book quiz — despite the fact that in production, they'd always have reference materials available. The result: new hires entering production underprepared, with no standardized way to measure readiness.

---

## My Approach

I designed a ground-up curriculum reimagination built on three principles:

### 1. Competency-Based, Not Topic-Based

Organized around what reviewers need to **do** — verify income, assess identity documents, evaluate application risk — rather than SOP titles. The difference sounds subtle; the curriculum impact is not.

### 2. Flipped Classroom

Learners review procedures before class. Class time is spent **performing the work** with documents available — the instructor as coach, not lecturer. This is how the job actually works; the training should reflect that.

### 3. Open-Resource, Performance-Based Assessments

Every assessment mirrors production conditions (full access to SOPs and tools), measuring whether learners can perform correctly with the resources they'll actually use — not whether they can recall procedures in isolation.

### Key Design Decisions

- **Extended the program from 2 to 3 weeks**, adding a capstone week with AI role-play simulations, complex practice scenarios, and manager observation
- **6 formal assessment gates** (vs. zero previously) with pass/fail consequences — no one enters production without demonstrated competency
- **114 modules across 7 curriculum stages** — modular architecture enabling reuse across new hire, upskill, cross-training, and refresher paths
- **Product-agnostic foundations taught once**, with product-specific procedures layered on top

## Outcomes

- ✅ Complete curriculum blueprint covering **100% of identified job responsibilities** and **100% of operational procedures**
- 🚦 **First formal assessment gates** for the role — no one enters production without demonstrated competency
- ♻️ Modular architecture enabled **~37% of new hire modules** to be directly reused in upskill and cross-training paths
- 📐 Flipped classroom model adopted as the **design standard for future curriculum builds** in the department
