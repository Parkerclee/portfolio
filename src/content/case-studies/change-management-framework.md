## Overview

An operations L&D team supporting ~20 business units had no systematic way to handle change requests. A small SOP tweak got the same treatment as a multi-BU product launch. Large changes were under-supported; small changes clogged the design queue. A 60-day audit revealed 11 specific gaps in the change-to-training pipeline. I built the framework to close them.

---

## The Problem

Every training request funneled through the same intake process regardless of size, urgency, or complexity. The result was a system that treated everything as equally important — which meant nothing was treated appropriately. Major product launches arrived without readiness materials. Minor procedure updates consumed disproportionate L&D bandwidth. There was no shared vocabulary between L&D and business stakeholders for scoping requests.

---

## My Approach

I built a **three-part change management framework**:

### 1. Training Solution Menu — 8-Tier Triage Matrix

Maps change types to appropriate training responses, from a simple notification (Tier 1) through a full curriculum build (Tier 8). Each tier includes estimated development time, recommended modalities, and real examples.

Changes are triaged on two dimensions:
- **Behavioral impact** — Does the agent need to do something differently?
- **Blast radius** — How many teams and roles are affected?

The matrix gave stakeholders a concrete, shared vocabulary: "This is a Tier 3 change" replaces the old dynamic of "we need training" arriving without context or scope.

### 2. Readiness Page Template

A standardized template for product launches and major changes, replacing the ad hoc mix of Slack messages, email threads, and tribal knowledge. Includes:
- Phased rollout tracking
- Member talking points
- Cross-BU impact mapping
- Post-launch feedback loops

### 3. Authoring Zones (Green / Yellow / Red)

A governance model defining who can create what training content:
- 🟢 **Green** — BU stakeholders can self-serve (notifications, job aids)
- 🟡 **Yellow** — Collaborative with L&D guidance
- 🔴 **Red** — L&D-owned (assessments, compliance content, full curricula)

Each zone includes templates and guardrails so self-service doesn't mean "anything goes."

---

## Outcomes

- 🗣️ Gave stakeholders a **shared vocabulary** for requesting training support — removing the friction of ambiguous scope conversations
- 📉 Reduced unnecessary intake tickets by giving BUs **self-serve options** for low-complexity changes
- 🔍 Readiness page template was used retroactively to audit two recent launches, surfacing systemic gaps that informed future planning
- 📖 Packaged as a **Change Management Learning Playbook** for the broader L&D team

---

## What This Shows

Systems thinking and process design beyond content creation. Stakeholder enablement: building tools that let others self-serve appropriately. Strategic L&D thinking: connecting training decisions to organizational change readiness. Business acumen: understanding that not every change needs a course.
